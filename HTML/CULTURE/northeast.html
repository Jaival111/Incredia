Unveiling The Rhythms

Behold The Elegant Dance Culture of North East India.

YAK CHHAM

The Yak Dance is a traditional dance form that depicts the yak, an animal integral to the lives of the people in the high-altitude regions of Sikkim and Arunachal Pradesh. Dancers wear masks and costumes that resemble yaks, complete with horns and furry bodies. The dance movements mimic the behavior of yaks and are performed to traditional music, celebrating the importance of this animal in the region's culture and economy.

BIHU

Bihu is the most famous dance form of Assam, performed during the Bihu festival, which marks the Assamese New Year and the arrival of spring. The dance is characterized by energetic and joyful movements, with both men and women participating. Dancers wear traditional Assamese attire, including colorful mekhela chadors and dhotis. Bihu dance is accompanied by traditional music played on instruments like the dhol, pepa (buffalo horn pipe), and gogona (jaw harp).

Thang Ta

Thang Ta is a traditional martial art dance form of Manipur, combining dance and combat techniques. It involves the use of swords (thang) and spears (ta) and showcases the agility, strength, and skill of the performers. The dance is accompanied by traditional music and is performed during festivals and cultural events.

SUA LUA

Chang Lo, also known as Sua Lua, is a traditional dance of the Chang tribe in Nagaland. It is performed during festivals and celebrations, characterized by energetic and rhythmic movements. Dancers wear traditional Naga attire, including colorful shawls and headgear adorned with feathers and beads. The dance is accompanied by traditional music and songs, reflecting the tribe's cultural heritage.

Northeast Indian cuisine is shaped by its diverse geography, with tropical and temperate climates, fertile hills, and abundant rivers providing a variety of local ingredients. The region’s culinary traditions reflect its ethnic diversity, with unique dishes influenced by neighboring countries like China, Myanmar, and Bangladesh. This results in a distinctive blend of indigenous spices, herbs, and cooking techniques such as steaming and fermenting.

STYLE
OF
FOOD

Northeast Indian cuisine features a style characterized by minimal use of oil, simple cooking methods like steaming and boiling, and a focus on natural flavors. It often includes fermented ingredients and relies on local herbs and spices used sparingly. Meals typically consist of rice paired with meats like pork, chicken, or fish, creating distinctive and bold flavor profiles.

STAPLE
FOODS
NORTH-EASTERN
INDIANS
SEEM

Northeast Indians commonly prefer rice as a staple food, often served with a variety of meats such as pork, chicken, and fish. They also enjoy dishes made with local vegetables, pulses, and fermented ingredients like bamboo shoots and fish. Meals frequently incorporate traditional flavors from indigenous herbs and spices, reflecting the region’s diverse culinary practices.

COOKING
OILS
COMMONLY
USED

In Northeast Indian cuisine, cooking oils are used sparingly, with mustard oil being the most commonly used. It adds a distinctive flavor to many dishes. Other oils, like sesame oil, are also used, but less frequently. The cuisine often relies more on natural flavors from fresh ingredients and herbs rather than extensive use of oil.

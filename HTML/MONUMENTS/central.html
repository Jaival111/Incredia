A Journey throguh Central India

KEDARNATH TEMPLE

Kedarnath Temple is one of the holiest Hindu shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located in the Himalayas near the Mandakini river in Kedarnath, it is part of the Char Dham Yatra and one of the twelve Jyotirlingas. The temple, believed to be constructed by the Pandavas, is renowned for its ancient architecture and religious significance.

TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal, situated in Agra, is an iconic symbol of love built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. This white marble mausoleum, completed in 1653, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Its exquisite beauty, intricate inlay work, and symmetrical gardens attract millions of visitors annually.

KHAJURAHO TEMPLES

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for its stunning temples adorned with intricate erotic sculptures. Built between 950 and 1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty, these temples are an exceptional example of Indian architecture and art. The complex originally contained 85 temples, of which about 25 survive today.

It is the widest waterfall in India and presents a spectacular sight, especially during the monsoon season. The surrounding natural beauty and the sound of the cascading water make it a popular tourist destination.
